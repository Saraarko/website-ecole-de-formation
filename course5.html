Course Overview

The Specialization includes two real-world projects: Big Networks and Genome Assembly. You will analyze both road networks and social networks and learn how to calculate the shortest path between New York and San Francisco 1000 times faster than the shortest path algorithms you learn in the standard Algorithms 101 course! Then, you will learn how to assemble genomes from millions of short DNA fragments and how assembly algorithms are powering recent advances in personalized medicine.

what you'll learn :

Apply algorithmic techniques (greedy algorithms, binary search, dynamic programming, etc.) and data structures (stacks, queues, trees, graphs, etc.) to solve 100 programming challenges that frequently appear in tech company interviews. You will receive instant feedback on whether your solution is correct.

Apply newly learned algorithms to solve real-world problems: navigate a large network or assemble the genome of a deadly pathogen from millions of short substrings of its DNA..

Build your GitHub portfolio by applying your skills to numerous labs and hands-on projects, including a capstone project.
